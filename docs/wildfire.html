Notes

Numerical approximation of PDE by Method of Lines (MOL) [1]. This method requires a RHS approximation, performed by finite difference ‘fd’ [2] or fast fourier transform ‘fft’ [3], and then solve an IVP using ‘Euler’ or ‘RK4’ method.

Variables used:

  • Temperature \(u(x,y,t)\) is represented by U.

  • Fuel \(\beta(x,y,t)\) is representeted by B.

  • Vector field with wind and topography \(\mathbf{v}(x,y,t)\) is represented by V.

The implementation needs to vectorize \(u\) and \(b\) into \(\mathbf{y}\), and then apply the time solver using the RHS function.
